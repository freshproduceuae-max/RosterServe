DEPARTMENT/SUB-TEAM ARCHITECTURAL DECISIONS (RS-F003)
- Hierarchy: event → department → sub-team (flat, no deeper nesting in v1)
- Owner assignment: department.owner_id references a dept_head profile; sub_team.owner_id references a sub_leader profile; both nullable (may be assigned after creation)
- Owner role invariant enforced at two layers: server-side verifyOwnerRole() in actions + BEFORE INSERT/UPDATE DB triggers in 00003_departments.sql
- Sub-leader rights in RS-F003: read-only. "Manage the structures they are responsible for" deferred to RS-F008+.
- Department management: super_admin only for create/edit/delete
- Sub-team management: super_admin OR owning dept_head (ownership verified server-side before mutation)
- Cascade soft-delete: enforced at three layers — action (cascade sub_teams on dept delete), RLS (dept_head sub_team SELECT joins departments with deleted_at IS NULL guard), query (getDepartmentsByEventId filters sub_teams.deleted_at IS NULL)
- Events RLS: pre-RS-F003 "Leaders can read active events" policy dropped; replaced with dept_head policy (events with owned dept) and sub_leader policy (events with owned sub_team)
- Profile visibility: 00004_leader_profile_read.sql adds a policy allowing dept_head/sub_leader to read other leader-role profiles (needed for owner dropdowns and display names)
- getOwnerDisplayNames(ids): targeted query for display purposes, available to all leader roles; getProfilesByRole(role): for owner-selection dropdowns, restricted to super_admin and dept_head callers
